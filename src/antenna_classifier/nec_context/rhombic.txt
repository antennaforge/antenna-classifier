Rhombic Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Four long wires forming a diamond (rhombus) shape, elevated above
ground. Terminated at the far end with a resistive load. Produces high gain
and very wide bandwidth on HF.

Key rules:
- Four GW cards forming a diamond in the horizontal plane at a fixed Z height.
- Feed at one vertex (apex), termination load at the opposite vertex.
- Leg length is typically 2-10λ.
- Apex half-angle typically 30-80° depending on design frequency.
- Termination resistor modelled with LD 0 card on the far-end wire.
- Dual feed: two EX cards at the feed vertex (one per wire meeting there),
  with opposite polarity.
- GE 1 with GN 1 for perfect ground.
