Turnstile Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Two dipoles crossed at 90° and fed 90° out of phase to produce
circular polarisation. Can be stacked for gain (stacked turnstile).

Key rules:
- Two horizontal dipole GW cards, perpendicular to each other, at the same height.
- Dipole 1 extends along X, dipole 2 extends along Y, both at the same Z.
- Feed both dipoles at their centres with 90° phase difference:
  EX on dipole 1: (1.0, 0.0) → 0° phase
  EX on dipole 2: (0.0, 1.0) → 90° phase
- Each dipole is ≈ λ/2 long.
- For stacking: repeat the pair at λ/2 vertical spacing.

Note: GW 1 along X-axis, GW 2 along Y-axis, both at Z=10m height.
Fed at centre segments (seg 11 of each). Phase quadrature: EX on GW 1 is
(1,0) and on GW 2 is (0,1) — producing right-hand circular polarisation.
For left-hand circular: use (0,-1) on GW 2.
