Fractal Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Antenna using fractal geometry (self-similar patterns) to achieve
multiband or wideband operation in a compact size. Common types include
Koch fractal dipoles, Sierpinski triangles, and Minkowski loops.

Key rules:
- Model the fractal shape as a series of short straight GW wires following
  the fractal pattern. Each iteration adds more detail.
- Wire endpoints connect sequentially to trace the fractal curve.
- Use enough segments per wire for accuracy (≥5 per section).
- Feed at the centre (for dipole-type fractals) or at the base.
- Fractal perimeter is longer than a straight dipole but fits in a smaller space.
- First iteration: simple shape. Higher iterations add bends.
