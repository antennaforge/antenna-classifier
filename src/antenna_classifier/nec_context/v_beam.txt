V-Beam Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Two long wires extending from a central feed point in a V shape.
The included angle determines the frequency and gain. Similar to a rhombic
but open at the far end (unterminated).

Key rules:
- Two long GW cards extending from a central feedpoint at a fixed Z height.
- The V opens symmetrically about the forward axis.
- V half-angle is typically 30-70° depending on leg length and frequency.
- Feed at the apex (junction of the two wires) — use a short connecting wire.
- GE 1 with GN for ground models.
- Use LD 5 for copper wire conductivity.
- Use GS for unit conversion if dimensions in feet.
