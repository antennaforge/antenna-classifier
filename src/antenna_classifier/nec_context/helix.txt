Helical Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Wire wound in a helix (spiral) along an axis. Axial-mode helices
produce circular polarisation and high gain. The helix diameter ≈ λ/π,
spacing between turns ≈ λ/4.

Key rules:
- Model the helix as many short straight GW segments following the spiral path.
- Each GW card is a short segment of the helix. Use ~12-20 segments per turn.
- Coordinates trace a 3D spiral: X = R*cos(θ), Y = R*sin(θ), Z increases linearly.
- Start with a short straight wire from ground to the helix start (feed point).
- Feed at the first segment (base of helix).
- GE 1 with GN for ground-plane models. Include a ground plane or radials.
- Use GS if dimensions are in inches.

Note: Each GW traces a short arc of the helix. X/Y coordinates follow a circle
of radius 4.35" while Z increases by pitch/segments_per_turn per wire. GS
converts inches to metres. For N turns with 20 segments/turn: ~20*N GW cards.
Radius R = D/2 where D ≈ λ/π. Pitch ≈ λ/4 per turn.
