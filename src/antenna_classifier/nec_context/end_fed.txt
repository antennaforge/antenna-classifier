End-Fed / Inverted-L Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Wire antenna fed at one end, often with a vertical section and a
horizontal top wire. The inverted-L has a vertical rise from ground and a
horizontal run. End-fed half-wave (EFHW) uses a matching transformer at the base.

Key rules:
- Vertical section: GW from ground up to support height.
- Horizontal section: GW extending horizontally from the top of the vertical.
- Feed at the base of the vertical section.
- Include ground radials (3-4 GW cards extending outward from the base).
- Total wire length ≈ λ/4 for an inverted-L, ≈ λ/2 for EFHW.
- Use GE 1 with GN 2 for real ground.
- Use GS for unit conversion if dimensions in feet.
