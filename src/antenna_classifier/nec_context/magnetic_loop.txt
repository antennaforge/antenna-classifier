Small Magnetic Loop (STL) Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Small loop (circumference << λ, typically λ/10 to λ/4) made of
thick conductor. Tuned with a variable capacitor. Very narrow bandwidth but
efficient for its tiny size.

Key rules:
- Square or octagonal loop using 4-8 GW cards, endpoints connecting to close the loop.
- Use thick wire radius (0.01m or more) — efficiency depends on conductor size.
- Loop circumference is much less than a wavelength.
- Add a tuning capacitor using LD 0 card (series RLC) on one wire segment.
  Set R=0, L=0, C=capacitance in Farads (e.g. 36.84pF).
- Feed opposite the capacitor.
- Use LD 5 for copper conductivity.

Example — Magnetic loop for 20 m (14.05 MHz), 1m sides:

Note: Four GW cards form a square loop in the X-Y plane at Z=20m.
1m sides, thick wire (0.01m radius). Tuning capacitor (LD 0, 36.84 pF)
on GW 3 at segment 5. Feed on GW 1, opposite the capacitor.
For smaller loops, reduce side length. Capacitance must be adjusted for resonance.
