Beverage Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: Very long horizontal wire close to the ground, terminated at the far
end with a resistive load. Used for receiving on low frequencies (80m, 160m).
Length is typically 1-4λ or more.

Key rules:
- Main wire: long horizontal GW at a fixed low height (1-3m / 6-10ft).
- Down-lead at feed end: vertical GW from ground to wire height.
- Termination: LD card (resistive load) at the far end of the wire.
- Optional "right-angle" configuration with perpendicular wire sections.
- Feed at the base of the down-lead.
- GE 1 with GN 2 for real ground (ground characteristics matter).
- Use GS for unit conversion if dimensions in feet.

Example — Beverage for 80 m (3.8 MHz), 600 ft long:

Note: GW 1 is the vertical down-lead from ground to Z=6ft. GW 2 is the main
horizontal wire (600ft along X at Z=6ft). GW 3-4 are right-angle termination
arms. LD terminates the main wire at its far end (seg 40) with 450 Ω.
GS converts feet to metres. Feed at base of down-lead (GW 1, seg 1).
