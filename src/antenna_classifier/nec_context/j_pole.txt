J-Pole Antenna — NEC Modeling Reference

Structure: End-fed half-wave vertical with a quarter-wave matching stub at the
base. Shaped like the letter "J" — a tall radiator with a shorter parallel
stub, connected at the bottom by a short horizontal wire.

Key rules:
- Main radiator: vertical GW from base to top (≈ 3/4λ total height).
- Matching stub: shorter parallel vertical GW, close to the radiator.
- Bottom connecting wire: short horizontal GW joining the two verticals at base.
- Feed at the short connecting wire at the base (or a tap point above base).
- The stub is ≈ λ/4 tall; the radiator extends ≈ λ/2 above the stub.
- Use GE 1 with GN for ground-plane models.

Note: GW 1 is the main radiator (vertical, Z=7.59 to Z=8.60m). GW 2 is the
lower section. GW 3-5 form the bottom U-bend connecting to the stub (GW 6).
GW 7 is the short horizontal feed wire. Feed on GW 7 (connecting wire).
Both verticals are parallel, separated by 0.01m in Y.
